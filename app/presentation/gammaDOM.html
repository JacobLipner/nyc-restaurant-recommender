PCA Dimensionality Reduction
We reduced 768-dimensional sentence embeddings to 128 dimensions using Incremental PCA. Achieved 99x speedup (locally) and 83% size reduction while retaining 75.5% of variance. Key insight: recall peaks at 128 and drops for higher counts, suggesting PCA acts as a noise filter.

Semantic Search
Our two-stage semantic search pipeline efficiently retrieves relevant restaurants by understanding natural language queries and leveraging pre-clustered data for rapid filtering.
This approach enables a 19x search space reduction, processing ~113K reviews instead of 2.1M for final ranking and up to 77x for niche queries.
For instance, the query "cheap ramen with fast service" matched clusters 19 (Ramen), 41 (Noodles), and 28 (Curry).

ABSA: From Stars to Aspects
A 4.5-star average doesn’t tell you whether it’s the food or the service driving that score.
Our model scores each aspect separately and auto-matches weights to your query.

Technical Pipeline & Ranking
1
Sentence split → clause split
2
Keyword match against 4 aspect dictionaries
3
VADER sentiment on each matched clause
4
Bayesian smoothing with global priors
Final ranking formula:
aspect_weighted=wfood⋅sfood+wservice⋅sservice+wprice⋅sprice+wwait⋅swait\text{aspect\_weighted} = w_{\text{food}} \cdot s_{\text{food}} + w_{\text{service}} \cdot s_{\text{service}} + w_{\text{price}} \cdot s_{\text{price}} + w_{\text{wait}} \cdot s_{\text{wait}}aspect_weighted=wfood​⋅sfood​+wservice​⋅sservice​+wprice​⋅sprice​+wwait​⋅swait​
Aspect weights are auto-extracted from query language (e.g. "cheap" → w_price ↑, "quick" → w_wait ↑).
